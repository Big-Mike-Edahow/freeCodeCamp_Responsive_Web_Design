Captain Loyal E. Birchell

Captain of the Utah State Penitentiary






Notable events in Captain Birchell's life:

  • 1915 - Born in Roosevelt, Utah
  • 1925 - He grew up on the Ouray Indian Reservation in northeastern Utah, going to school with members of the Ute Indian Tribe. As a young boy, he would quite often ride his horse out into the desert, tending to his family's livestock.
  • 1936 - Leaves the family ranch to work at the Kennecott Copper Mine, one of the worlds largest open pit mines, in West Jordan, Utah
  • 1939 - Marries wife of 50 years, Virginia Marie Wixom. She passed away at the age of 73 in 1989 from a stroke.
  • 1945 - Serves in the United States Army during world War II. He was stationed on the island of Okinawa in the Pacific Ocean. He brought home an old Japanese rifle that he had found on the island as a keepsake.
  • 1950 - Started working at the Utah State Penitentiary, where he worked for a total of 25 years. He rose through the ranks to become Captain of the Prison, one step below the Warden.
  • 1975 - Retires from the prison and works for ten years as a security guard for Hercules Corporation, a defense contractor that made rocket motor propellant among other aerospace work.
  • 2011 - Passed away at the age of 96 at the Bingham Memorial Hospital Extended Care Facility in Blackfoot, Idaho.
